# Require Evidence Boundaries Without a Fixed Limitations Section

Every completed Research Narrative has an Argument Responsibility to make the limits and applicability of its claims discoverable, but the Living Manuscript does not require a fixed `局限性与适用边界` or Limitations section. The boundary account distinguishes limitations supported by observed evidence from conditions that were not evaluated and generalizations that remain unknown. It may not invent generic risks, present an untested condition as a demonstrated failure, or imply that missing evidence supports a broader claim.

The Manuscript Sculptor places this account where it best fits the contribution and any future venue requirements: for example in a dedicated Limitations section, Discussion, Threats to Validity, Safety or Ethics analysis, Conclusion, or another mandated location. For theoretical work, assumptions and theorem scope may carry the responsibility; for empirical work, evaluated conditions and explicit generalization gaps may carry it. Final validation checks that the responsibility is fulfilled and evidence-aligned, not that a particular heading exists.

This supersedes ADR-0008's mandatory top-level limitations clause and refines ADR-0051's Adaptive Argument Structure.

**Considered Options**

- Keep Limitations as a mandatory top-level section. Rejected because contribution types and venue rules place boundary arguments differently.
- Make limitations optional whenever evidence is sparse. Rejected because untested conditions and unknown generalization are themselves necessary boundaries on the paper's claims.
- Ask the Manuscript Sculptor to generate plausible generic limitations. Rejected because plausible prose without supporting evidence creates false scientific claims rather than honest disclosure.
