# Optimize for Argument Density, Not Record Completeness

Argument Density is the leading editorial principle of the Manuscript Sculptor Prompt. Every retained paragraph, equation, citation, figure, and table must perform a discernible function in the central scientific argument, such as establishing a claim, presenting evidence, explaining a result, positioning prior work, or delimiting what the evidence supports. Factual correctness is necessary but does not justify content whose only function is to preserve project history or demonstrate that work occurred.

The sculptor removes generic background, repeated summaries, raw result inventories, procedural narration, and technically true side material when they do not help a reader understand or assess the contribution. This principle does not demand maximal brevity: definitions, setup, derivations, counterexamples, and detailed analysis remain when the argument needs them. The prompt does not impose a paragraph formula, a permitted-function enumeration, a word limit, or a compression score; the model judges argumentative contribution directly.

This refines ADR-0060's outcome criterion and unifies the publication-oriented exclusions in ADR-0052, ADR-0053, and ADR-0055. ADR-0068 applies the same principle to early manuscript growth: missing evidence produces absence rather than filler.

**Considered Options**

- Preserve all factually correct research material. Rejected because a publication manuscript is a selective argument rather than a project archive.
- Require every paragraph to follow a fixed claim-evidence-explanation template. Rejected because theoretical exposition, definitions, comparisons, and transitions require different rhetorical forms.
- Maximize concision. Rejected because density is the ratio of argumentative value to content, not a preference for the shortest possible paper.
